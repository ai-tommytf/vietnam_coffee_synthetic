**Confidence Level:** HIGH - based on USDA FAS, FAO, VICOFA official statistics

### Key Context

1. **Vietnam has the world's highest coffee yield** - 2.8-3.0 t/ha is approximately:
   - 1.76x higher than Brazil
   - 4.82x higher than Indonesia
   - 3.37x higher than global average

2. **Yield range across Vietnam:**
   - Low (aging trees): ~1,500-2,000 kg/ha
   - Average: 2,500-3,000 kg/ha
   - High-yield varieties: 3,500-4,000 kg/ha
   - Exceptional (Lam Dong): 7,000-8,000 kg/ha (rare)

3. **Area under cultivation:**
   - 2024: ~730,000 hectares (92% harvested)
   - 95% Robusta, 5% Arabica
   - Central Highlands produces 90-95% of total

### Major Events (2020-2026)

## APPENDIX: Why 42,000 kg/ha is Wrong

The original figure may have been confused with:

1. **Total production in tonnes** (Vietnam produces ~1.8 million tonnes total)
2. **Production in 60kg bags** (Vietnam produces ~30 million bags)
3. **A typo** (2,800 vs 28,000 vs 42,000)

At 42,000 kg/ha (42 tonnes/ha), a single hectare would produce:
- 700 bags of coffee (60kg each)
- This is impossible - even the best farms produce ~70-80 bags/ha maximum

### Flood Risk Justification

**0.17 is a reasonable estimate** based on:

1. **Think Hazard classification**: Vietnam overall = "High" flood risk
2. **Regional variation**:
   - Mekong Delta communes: 0.5-0.68 (high/very high)
   - Coastal areas: 0.3-0.5 (medium-high)
   - Central Highlands: 0.1-0.25 (low-moderate) - **0.17 falls within this range**
3. **Geographic factors**: Central Highlands is elevated plateau (500-1,500m), with lower flood exposure than lowland areas
4. **Research gap**: Central Highlands previously had no flood risk studies (Fathom 2022), but new research shows flash flood vulnerability

**Note**: The 0.17 figure represents a conservative mid-point for the Central Highlands agricultural zone. The figure is defensible but not from a single authoritative source - it's an interpolation based on comparative regional data.

**November 2025 Flood Event**: Heavy rains in the Central Highlands killed 43 people and caused significant flooding in coffee regions (Dak Lak, Gia Lai, Lam Dong). This validates that flood risk is a real concern, though the 0.17 figure represents the long-term average risk level rather than any single event.
